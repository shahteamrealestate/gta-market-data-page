Common questions

GTA housing market data — FAQ

What does GTA housing market data tell buyers?

It helps buyers understand price trends, inventory, days on market and how much choice may be available in different cities. This can help you decide where to search, how quickly to act and whether there may be room to negotiate. A market with more listings than buyers typically gives buyers more time and more leverage on price.

What does market data tell sellers?

It helps sellers understand competition, buyer activity and pricing pressure. If inventory is rising or homes are taking longer to sell, pricing, preparation and presentation become more important. Homes that are priced accurately sell faster and for more than overpriced homes that sit and get reduced.

Is Brampton a buyer's market or seller's market right now?

That depends on inventory, days on market, property type and price range. A city-wide number is useful, but a detached home, townhouse and condo can behave very differently in the same city at the same time. Use the dashboard above to compare specific types, then ask Team Shah Real Estate to review your specific situation.

How should I compare average price, inventory and days on market?

Do not look at one number alone. Average price shows the broad direction of the market. Inventory shows how much choice buyers have — more inventory generally means more room to negotiate. Days on market shows how quickly homes are moving. Together these three numbers give a much more realistic picture than any one of them alone.

Should I use market data before making an offer?

Yes. Market data helps you understand whether a home is priced aggressively, fairly or high compared with current conditions in that city and property type. Your offer strategy should also consider recent comparable sales, property condition, how long the home has been listed, and your own financing comfort. Ask Gaurang to help you read the data for a specific property before you write an offer.

How often is this data updated?

The dashboard pulls from TRREB Market Watch, which publishes monthly. Conditions can shift meaningfully from month to month depending on sales activity, new listings, and economic signals like Bank of Canada rate decisions. Always confirm the most recent numbers before relying on them for a purchase or sale decision.
